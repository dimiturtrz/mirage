# Edge-VLA — taking the heaviest policy to the edge: distill 50→1 step, int8-quantize, measure the cost

**Date:** 2026-07-18 · **Epic:** hxq (child hxq.4) · **Code:** `control/edge.py` · **Run:** `python -m control.edge`

## Question

The control leg's stated differentiator is **edge-VLA**: a policy that runs real-time on-device,
demonstrable sim-in-the-loop without hardware. The diffusion policy (hxq.2) is the worst-case graph for the
edge — it produces each action by running a **50-step reverse denoising chain**, i.e. 50 network evaluations
per control step. This asks the direct question: can that graph be taken to an edge-deployable form, and what
does deployability cost in task success? Two levers, both measured: **distillation** (collapse the 50-step
sampler into one feed-forward pass) and **int8 quantization** (the weight form a fixed-function NPU runs).

## Method

- **Teacher.** The trained diffusion policy (conditional DDPM over K=8 action chunks, 50 steps).
- **Distillation.** `DistilledPolicy` is a 1-step MLP student that regresses the *teacher's sampled action
  chunk* over 3000 demonstration observations — standard diffusion→one-step distillation at toy scale. It
  satisfies `core.policy.ControlPolicy`, so inference drops from 50 network evaluations to **1** while it
  still rolls through the unchanged spine.
- **Quantization.** Dynamic int8 quantization of the student's linear layers (`EdgeVLA.quantize`), then the
  quantized graph is rolled in sim/real to show the task survives the edge form.
- **Footprint.** Parameter count + the serialized graph size (`torch.save` of the state dict); step-count is
  the latency proxy (latency ∝ steps × per-step FLOPs, and the per-step nets are comparable). **Op-class** is
  the deploy explorer's fit axis — dense ships anywhere, an iterative denoiser pays latency × steps, attention
  doesn't compile to a fixed NPU.
- **Eval.** 200 matched-seed episodes in nominal sim and the headline +50% payload / −10% actuator real;
  `gap = success(sim) − success(real)`. Single seed, matching the paradigm compare.

## Result (single seed, +50% payload)

| policy | steps | params (k) | size (kB) | op-class | sim | real | gap (pp) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|
| diffusion (teacher) | 50 | 37.9 | 151.1 | iterative_dense | 100.0% | 27.0% | 73.0 |
| distilled (fp32) | 1 | 19.2 | 77.8 | dense | 100.0% | 23.0% | 77.0 |
| distilled (int8) | 1 | 19.2 | 24.2 | dense | 100.0% | 22.5% | 77.5 |

## Interpretation — a 50× inference cut and a 6× smaller graph, for +4.5 pp of gap

**Distillation collapses inference 50→1 and halves the graph, at a small quality cost.** The 1-step student
runs a single forward pass instead of the teacher's 50-step chain (a **50× cut in network evaluations** — the
dominant latency term), on **half the parameters** (37.9k → 19.2k) and **half the disk** (151 → 78 kB). Sim
success is fully retained (100%); the real-shift success drops 27% → 23%, a **+4 pp sim-to-real gap** for the
50× speedup. That is the honest distillation trade — one-step students lose a little of the teacher's
robustness — measured, not hidden.

**int8 quantization is nearly free here.** It shrinks the student **3.2× on disk** (77.8 → 24.2 kB) while the
task barely moves (real 23.0% → 22.5%, gap +0.5 pp). The end product is a **24 kB, single-pass, dense-op-class
policy** — small enough for on-die NPU SRAM, one forward per control step, no iterative sampling.

**The op-class verdict crosses the deploy explorer — and repeats its finding.** The three paradigms fail the
edge in the *same* ways the perception detectors do:
- the **diffusion teacher** is dense per-step but **iterative** — 50× the latency, edge-prohibitive against a
  30 fps inline budget;
- **ACT** (paradigm compare) is **attention** — the same op-class the Point-MAE 3D detector carries, which
  does not compile to a fixed-function NPU (Coral/Hailo);
- the **distilled+quantized student** is **dense** and single-pass — the op-class that ships on any accelerator,
  fixed NPUs included.

So the edge-VLA answer is concrete: **an attention or iterative policy does not reach a fixed NPU as-is; distill
it to a dense single-pass student and quantize** — the graph then fits the same conv-native lane the deployable
perception detectors live in. That is the differentiator demonstrated end-to-end in sim, no hardware required.

## Integrity

- **A projection of the edge, not a measured device.** Footprints are params + serialized size + step-count;
  step-count is a latency *proxy*, not a measured on-device FPS. The measured-hardware bench (ONNX → TensorRT,
  real latency) is the deploy leg's hardware-gated bead (0sq) — the same honesty stance as the deploy explorer
  (an analytical projection, flagged as such).
- **Single seed — directional.** One training seed, matching the paradigm compare; the +4 pp distillation cost
  and +0.5 pp quantization cost are one-seed point estimates. Multi-seed hardening is filed (7wz).
- **Op-class fit stated, not unified.** The op-class verdict is named control-locally (import-linter keeps
  control independent of `surfscan.deploy`); folding policies into the *shared* deploy fit matrix is the
  substrate generalization (j5s.6), which this graph is the input to.
- **Quantization API.** `torch.ao.quantization.quantize_dynamic` is deprecated for torch ≥ 2.10 (migrates to
  torchao); kept here as it is present and standard in the pinned torch, not worth a new dependency now.
